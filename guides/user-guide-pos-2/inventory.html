Inventory Management

Manage all your products, categories, brands, and measurement units from the inventory section.

Products

The Products module allows you to add, edit, view, and manage all your inventory items.

Adding a New Product

  1. Navigate to Inventory ‚Üí Products
  2. Click the "Add Product" button
  3. Fill in the product details:
    • Product Name (required)
    • SKU/Barcode
    • Category
    • Brand
    • Measurement Unit
    • Cost Price
    • Selling Price
    • Minimum Stock Level
    • Description
  4. Click "Save" to create the product
Unit conversion: If your items move through different units, select the Purchase, Transfer, and Sales units and set the conversion rates so stock and pricing remain consistent across modules.
  • Purchase ‚Üí Transfer rate: How many transfer units are in one purchase unit (e.g., 1 box = 12 packs ‚Üí enter 12).
  • Transfer ‚Üí Sales rate: How many sales units are in one transfer unit (e.g., 1 pack = 6 pieces ‚Üí enter 6).

Example: with 1 box = 12 packs and 1 pack = 6 pieces, the system knows 1 box = 72 pieces. GRN uses purchase units, transfers use transfer units, and POS uses sales units; quantities are auto-converted between them.

Viewing Product List

The products list displays all your inventory items with the following information:

  • Id
  • Bar Code
  • Product name
  • Category and brand
  • Purchase Price
  • Selling price
  • Quantity
  • Status
  • Actions (Edit, Duplicate, View Details)
üí° Tip: Use the search and filter options to quickly find specific products in large inventories.

Stock Quantity Management Logic

The system automatically manages product quantities across two locations (Store and Shop) based on different operations:

Store Quantity:
  • GRN (Goods Received Note): When you add a GRN, the store quantity increases ‚úÖ
  • GRN Return: When you return goods via GRN return, the store quantity decreases ‚¨áÔ∏è
  • Product Release Note: When you release products from store to shop, the store quantity decreases ‚¨áÔ∏è
Shop Quantity:
  • Product Release Note: When you receive products from store, the shop quantity increases ‚úÖ
  • Sales: When you make a sale, the shop quantity decreases ‚¨áÔ∏è
  • Sales Return: When a customer returns items, the shop quantity increases ‚úÖ
Note: All quantity adjustments happen automatically when you process the respective transactions. You don't need to manually update stock levels.

Categories

Organize your products into categories for better management and reporting.

Managing Categories

  1. Go to Inventory ‚Üí Categories
  2. Click "Add Category"
  3. Enter category name and select parent category.
  4. Create category

Brands

Manage product brands to categorize items by manufacturer or supplier brand.

Adding Brands

  1. Go to Inventory ‚Üí Brands
  2. Click "Add Brand"
  3. Enter brand name
  4. Add brand logo or description (optional)
  5. Save

Product Types

Manage product types for additional categorization.

Adding Product Types

  1. Go to Inventory ‚Üí Types
  2. Click "Add Type"
  3. Enter type name
  4. Save

Measurement Units

Define measurement units (kg, pieces, liters, etc.) for accurate stock tracking.

Adding Measurement Units

  1. Go to Inventory ‚Üí Units
  2. Click "Add Unit"
  3. Enter unit name (e.g., "Pieces", "Kg", "Liters")
  4. Enter abbreviation (e.g., "pcs", "kg", "L")
  5. Save
Common Units:
  • pcs - Pieces
  • kg - Kilograms
  • L - Liters
  • box - Box
  • pack - Pack
  • bag - Bag
  • meter - Meter
